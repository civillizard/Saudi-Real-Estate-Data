## 1. LEGISLATIVE FOUNDATION

### Original Law (2015–2016)
- **Royal Decree No. M/4** — issued 24 November 2015 (1 Safar 1437H)
- **Council of Ministers Decision No. 377** — 13 June 2016
- Established the White Land Fee Law (نظام رسوم الأراضي البيضاء)
- Official gazette PDF: https://momah.gov.sa/sites/default/files/2025-01/nzam%20rswm%20ala'rady%20albyda.pdf

### 2025 Amendment (Major Overhaul)
- **Council of Ministers Resolution No. 758** — 29 April 2025
- **Royal Decree No. M/244** — 12 May 2025 (publication date)
- Law renamed: **"White Land and Vacant Real Estate Tax Law"** (نظام رسوم الأراضي البيضاء والعقارات الشاغرة)
- Implementing Regulations published in Official Gazette (Umm Al-Qura): **22 August 2025**
- Official gazette: https://uqn.gov.sa/details?p=27369
- BOE (Bureau of Experts): https://laws.boe.gov.sa/BoeLaws/Laws/LawDetails/6932e745-0f61-4f25-9e4a-a9a700f21d6b/1

## 2. FEE RATES

### Pre-2025 System (2016–2025)
- **Flat rate: 2.5% per year** of assessed land market value
- No tiered zones — one national rate applied uniformly
- Applied only to undeveloped land designated for residential or commercial use

### Post-2025 System (August 2025 onward)
#### White Land (Undeveloped)
Five-tier zone system based on urban development priority. Rate is 0–10% of land value annually:

| Tier | Priority Level | Annual Rate |
|------|---------------|-------------|
| Tier 1 | Highest (أولوية قصوى) | **10%** of land value |
| Tier 2 | High (أولوية عالية) | **7.5%** of land value |
| Tier 3 | Medium (أولوية متوسطة) | **5%** of land value |
| Tier 4 | Low (منخفضة الأولوية) | **2.5%** of land value |
| Tier 5 | Outside priority areas | **0%** (exempt, but counted toward total area) |

#### Vacant Real Estate (Buildings) — NEW in 2025
- Annual fee: **up to 5% of assessed rental value** (أجرة المثل)
- Can be raised to **up to 10%** by Council of Ministers recommendation
- Applies to developed/built properties that remain unoccupied without justification
- Implementing regulations for vacant buildings due within **1 year** of May 2025 publication (i.e., by ~May 2026)
- As of March 2026: detailed vacant property regulations not yet issued

## 3. GEOGRAPHIC SCOPE & SIZE THRESHOLDS

### Size Threshold
| Period | Threshold |
|--------|-----------|
| 2016–2024 (Phases 1 & 2) | 10,000 m² or more per owner per city |
| 2024–2025 (Phase 3) | 5,000 m² or more (single parcel or aggregated) |
| 2025 onward (new law) | **5,000 m²** (single or aggregated across parcels in the same city) |

**Aggregation rule:** Multiple smaller plots under one owner within the same city are combined. If total >= 5,000 m², all qualify for the fee.

### Location Criteria
Land must be:
1. Located within the declared **urban boundary** (حدود النطاق العمراني) of a covered city
2. Classified as undeveloped/vacant under applicable laws
3. Capable of development (infrastructure available: roads, water, electricity, sewage)
4. Subject to a ministerial announcement (الإعلان) designating that city

### Geographic Zone Definition
- Each covered city is divided into geographic zones published on the MOMAH portal with maps
- Zones are determined by a **technical committee of licensed appraisers**
- Ministry reviews zones annually and can adjust rates, zone boundaries, or suspend the program
- Riyadh zone maps: https://idlelands.momah.gov.sa/ar/media-center/85966

## 4. CITY ROLLOUT — CHRONOLOGICAL TIMELINE

### Phase 1: Initial Four Cities (2016–2017)
**Scope:** Undeveloped plots >= 10,000 m²
**Fee:** 2.5% flat

| City | First Billing Date |
|------|--------------------|
| Riyadh (الرياض) | ~30 March 2017 |
| Jeddah (جدة) | ~April 2017 |
| Dammam metro / Eastern Province (حاضرة الدمام) | ~April–May 2017 |
| Makkah (مكة المكرمة) | 2017 |

**Results from this phase:**
- ~SR 2.2 billion+ collected for infrastructure
- 80+ residential housing projects funded
- 185,000+ Saudi families provided housing units/land
- 500 million m² of land registered in program across all four cities
- ~8,000 payment orders issued since start

### Expansion: 17 New Cities (2020–2021)
**Announced:** September 9, 2020

**First 5 cities (by end of 2020 — Phase 1 in new cities):**
1. Al-Madinah Al-Munawwarah (المدينة المنورة)
2. Abha (أبها)
3. Khamis Mushait (خميس مشيط) — part of Asir metro (حاضرة عسير)
4. Al-Taif (الطائف)
5. Jizan/Jazan (جازان)

**Remaining 12 cities:** phased during 2021 (specific names not confirmed from available sources — program covered cities including Tabuk, Hail, Qassim region cities, among others)

**Phase 2 in original four cities (announced ~2020–2021):**
- Scope: Developed plots >= 10,000 m² for single owner in one approved master plan
- Cities: Riyadh, Jeddah, Dammam simultaneously
- Then expanded to Makkah, Jeddah, Dammam (Phase 2 + expanded Phase 1 scope)
- Covered more than **190 residential neighborhoods** across the three cities

### 2021 Regulatory Amendment
- Executive Regulations amended (Cabinet Decision No. 379/1437 revised)
- Formalized three-phase framework:
  - Phase 1: Undeveloped, >= 10,000 m², in designated zones
  - Phase 2: Single-owner developed land >= 10,000 m² in one approved plan
  - Phase 3: Developed land >= 5,000 m² where single owner holds >= 10,000 m² total in one city
- Ministry gains power to apply multiple phases in same city simultaneously, skip phases, or suspend

### 2022–2024: Continued Billing Cycles
- Program continued billing in "sessions" within the same phase/city
- January 2023: 7th session of Phase 1, Riyadh — targeting >= 10,000 m² undeveloped
- Concurrent 2nd phase billing in Jeddah, Makkah, Dammam
- **Q1 2024 milestone:** Over 332 million m² billed across Phases 1 and 2
- Phase 1 expansion to new cities including Hail (حائل) and Qassim region (منطقة القصيم)

### 2025 Major Overhaul
- May 2025: New law enacted, renamed, vacants added, rate raised to up to 10%
- August 22, 2025: New implementing regulations published in Official Gazette
- August 2025: Five-tier zone system for Riyadh announced with maps
- Regulations effective immediately on publication (August 22, 2025)

### 2026: First Billing Under New System
- **1 January 2026:** First invoicing under the new five-tier system in Riyadh
- **Over 60,000 invoices** issued to landowners in Riyadh in the first wave
- Threshold reduced to >= 5,000 m² (down from 10,000 m²)
- Rates now 2.5%–10% depending on zone (vs. flat 2.5% before)
- Other cities (Jeddah, Makkah, Dammam, etc.): expected to follow under new system; exact dates not yet confirmed as of this writing

## 6. VACANT PROPERTY FEES (رسوم العقارات الشاغرة)

### Status as of March 2026
The 2025 law (Royal Decree M/244) introduced the concept, but **detailed implementing regulations have not yet been published.**
- Regulations due: within one year of May 12, 2025 = by ~May 2026
- Monitoring source for announcement: https://idlelands.momah.gov.sa/ar and https://laws.boe.gov.sa

### Known Framework (from the law itself)
- **Applies to:** Built/developed properties within urban boundaries that remain unoccupied without legal justification, and whose vacancy affects real estate market supply
- **Rate:** Annual fee of up to **5% of assessed rental equivalent** (أجرة المثل)
- **Maximum:** Can be raised to **10%** by Council of Ministers decision
- **Exclusions:** State-owned properties
- **Definition of "vacant":** Buildings remaining unused for extended periods without acceptable justification (exact duration to be specified in regulations)
- **Scope:** Residential and commercial buildings within declared urban zones

### Key Difference: White Land vs. Vacant Property
| Dimension | White Land (أراضي بيضاء) | Vacant Real Estate (عقارات شاغرة) |
|-----------|--------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Asset type | Undeveloped bare land | Built structures (buildings) |
| Fee basis | % of land market value | % of rental equivalent value |
| Max rate | 10% | 5% (up to 10% by Cabinet) |
| Size threshold | >= 5,000 m² | Not yet specified |
| Regulations status | Issued August 2025 | Pending (due by May 2026) |

## 7. EXEMPTIONS & DEFERRALS

A property is exempt from or may defer the white land fee if:
1. **Development completed** within the statutory payment period (fee is waived/refunded)
2. **Legal impediment** exists preventing permit issuance (must be beyond owner's control)
3. **Government-owned property** (excluded from the law)
4. **Outside declared zones** (Tier 5 — but area still counted in ownership totals)
5. **Development plan approved** and building permit issued within deadline
6. **Application for grace period** submitted with credible development plan and supporting documentation

**Penalty for non-compliance:** Fine up to **100% of the fee value**
**Property transfer restriction:** All accrued fees must be settled before a property title deed can be transferred/sold

---

## 8. ENFORCEMENT & COMPLIANCE

- **Invoice payment deadline:** 1 calendar year from invoice date (new invoices); 90 days for backdated invoices
- **Appeal deadline:** 60 days from invoice notification to file objection with competent committee
- **Committee response time:** Must issue decision within 60 days of receiving objection
- **Revenue allocation:** All fees deposited to a dedicated account at Saudi Central Bank (SAMA), allocated exclusively to housing projects
- **Property sale restriction:** Cannot transfer title deed until all fees are paid
- **Developer support:** "Etmam" (إتمام) Developers Services Center — unified platform for master plan approvals, building permits, and licensing

## 11. KEY OPEN QUESTIONS (as of March 2026)

1. **Other cities' 2026 billing dates:** When will Jeddah, Makkah, Dammam, and other cities receive invoices under the new five-tier rate system? Not yet officially announced.

2. **Vacant property (building) regulations:** Exact definitions, thresholds, rates, and enforcement mechanism for the new vacant building fee. Due by May 2026.

3. **Full list of 17 expansion cities:** Only 5 were named publicly (Madinah, Abha, Khamis Mushait, Taif, Jizan). The other 12 were never individually announced in available public sources.

4. **Zone tier maps for cities other than Riyadh:** Five-tier maps have been published for Riyadh; maps for Jeddah, Makkah, Dammam under new system not confirmed.

5. **REGA's role:** The **Real Estate General Authority (REGA; الهيئة العامة للعقار)** is involved in real estate regulation broadly but the White Land Tax program is administered by MOMAH. REGA's specific involvement in WLT enforcement is unclear.
